Epicenter Education · Student Flows

Full Student Flow Storyboard — In PRD Order

The counterpart to the counsellor storyboard, from the student's side of the same product. Static screens only — nothing here is clickable or functional. Visual language inspired by the reference dashboard supplied, adapted into the product's established light theme rather than copied directly.

📌 The PRD's numbered Use Cases (§4) are written from the counsellor's point of view — there's no equivalent numbered list for the student. This storyboard's ordering is derived instead from PRD §3 Users, §5 User Stories, and the Permissions detail throughout §8 Product Requirements, arranged into the same chronological arc the counsellor journey follows.
Where a screen here is the other side of a counsellor screen already shown (Kabir's onboarding, Ananya's shortlist suggestion, Ananya's essay feedback), the same student and situation is reused on purpose, so the two documents read as one consistent product.
✨ v3 update: a full 6-step onboarding wizard (age, grade, subjects, hobbies, major, EC list) is now woven into SU1, between first login and the first Home dashboard view — with AI extracting structured tags from free-text answers. AI note clean-up (lighter version) was added to SU5. "Send to Rohan" is now "Send to Counsellor" throughout. Look for the small AI badge.

Design choices worth flagging

  • Tick-then-confirm everywhere. Roadmap/To-Do tasks and application requirements now follow the same pattern: the student marks something done or submitted, and it only counts as Complete once the counsellor confirms it.
  • Expanded profile. Extracurriculars & Achievements and Test Scores & Academic History are now part of both the student's own profile editing and the counsellor's view of it.
  • Evidence uploads show a real visual. Roadmap task evidence now previews as an actual thumbnail rather than a generic document placeholder.
  • Shortlist priorities, not a suggester. Ananya can record what she wants out of her university list; it's a reference card for Rohan, not an automated recommendation engine.
  • Pop-ups now open centered. Every panel in this document opens in the middle of the screen instead of pinned to the top right.
  • Forms stay contextual. A new form shows up as a to-do item on Home, the same way Documents live inside Roadmap/Application context rather than getting their own nav tab.
  • No separate Documents page. The reference nav has no "Documents" item, and neither does this storyboard — uploads happen contextually inside Roadmap tasks and Application requirements, matching the counsellor document's stance that documents belong to what they support, not to a standalone tab.
  • Gamification omitted. The reference dashboard's streak and points icons aren't a described PRD feature, so they were left out rather than invented. Everything shown ties back to a real requirement.
  • Recommendations stay status-only. The PRD marks the full recommendation module (questionnaire, teacher portal) Post-MVP — students see a status pill inside My Application (as shown in Student Journey 6), not a brag-sheet flow, consistent with that scoping.
  • Onboarding replaces cold profile fields, not the discovery conversation. SU1's wizard captures age/grade/subjects/hobbies/major/EC list on day one; SU2 is now specifically what only comes out of an actual meeting with a counsellor (preferred countries, career interest, test scores) — the two no longer ask for the same thing twice.
  • AI badge is permanent, not a "needs review" flag. Once a cleaned note, an extracted onboarding tag, or a normalized major is saved, the small AI marker stays — it's a provenance record, not a to-do.
  • Four screens double as both sides of the same moment. Suggesting Cornell, and the essay feedback loop, are the same events already shown from Rohan's side in the counsellor storyboard — shown here from Ananya's side, on purpose, so the two documents describe one product rather than two.
